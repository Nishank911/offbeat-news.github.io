What is Offbeat News?

News is full of positive and negative vibes. It’s really up to us to look at the greener side and start our days with a tint of a smile.

Good things do happen every day, yet go unnoticed. Today is never late to change this perspective and join our journey on the path to optimism through happy news. So, it is of paramount importance that you make this choice today.

‘Offbeat News’ is a smart news aggregator App that brings optimistic, insightful, and solution-oriented news at your finger-tips. It uses positive journalism that channelizes you to a better path towards optimism than that portrayed in mainstream media.

Smart curation of news done in the Offbeat News app filters out all the negative news using customized tools and brings positive news to the user daily. The diversity of the positive news covered ranges from global to local.

To create media for the future it collaborates with various Schools, NGOs, Psychologists, Mental Health Pages, and influencers to help spread awareness about how bingeing on positive news is advantageous to one. The initiative is also creating a community on its social media platform and urges everyone to be a part and spread positivity.

The App also provides various features to help seamless user-interaction. It follows minimal display and is customizable to the core. It helps to search, discover, and follow users’ interests and shows news accordingly. It provides saving and sharing options to help spread positivity. Offbeat News also notifies the user about his favourite topics on users’ preferred social media platform.

Offbeat News uses five different Artificial Intelligence models to yield an effective user experience. Let’s dive deeper into them.

1. Summarization Model

The extractive summarization approach selects several news segments from the source to make up a summary of 60-80 words. It works as a highlighter underlining the important news.

2. Category Classification Model

The category classification model predicts the best possible category to which the news belongs. The news is mapped to multiple categories as per their relevance.

3. Tag Detection Model

The Tag Detection Model searches for important keywords in the news, to help us identify users' likings. These likings are learned by the system to provide the user with preferred categorical news.

4. Recommendation Model

The content-based recommendation model learns the preference each user would give to particular news in a couple of days and customizes news accordingly for every user. It suggests news relevant to the user by keeping a track of tags present in the news, the user has read.

5. Relevance Model

The relevance model ranks the news based on maximum relevance to the news category, sentiment score, and lexical diversity. Onboard this journey with us to help create media for the future.
